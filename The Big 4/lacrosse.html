LACROSSE

 

 

Lacrosse was orignially a tribal game played by eastern Woodlands Native Americans and by some Indian tribes. This sport was brought into the United States and Canada but was modified. The game began with the ball being tossed into the air and people would try to catch it. Passing the ball and dodging an opponent was seen as a trick and for the higher leveled players. Now, lacrosse is considered one of the fastest growing sports around the world. Traditional lacrosse games could last for several days and as many as 100 to 1,000 men from opposing villages or tribes would participate. The games were played in open plains between the two villages with large goals. Rules for these games were decided on the day before. There was no out of bounds and the ball could be touched with the hands.
