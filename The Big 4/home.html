Where Our Lady Lynx Are Now

Selena Pruitt Morgan Smith Lauren Avant

Selena Pruitt '17 a track and basketball superstar, has now moved on to bigger and better things. Selena is now a lady charger at the University of Alabama Huntsville. During her time at Lausanne she was nominated for Division 2-A Miss Basketball her senior year. She was named to All-Region team in 2014, 2015, and 2016. Overall in her caareer she scored over 2,000 points, along with averaging 20.2 points per game.

Morgan Smith ’19 started her lacrosse career in fifth grade and got the opportunity to play on the middle school and jv team at Collierville High School. When Morgan's love for the sport grew, she decided to quit soccer and cross country in the sixth grade to focus on lacrosse. She played every position, including goalie, and got chosen to play for the No Excuse Club Lacrosse Team. Morgan experienced an obstacle in the eighth grade when her first lacrosse coach passed due to a heart attack and tore her ACL causing her to miss the post season. After seven months of rehab, she was selected as the only freshman to start on varsity at Houston High School. She also made the all state team for a national tournament in the ninth and tenth grade. At the national tournament, Morgan got fifth in the state in her ninth grade year and seventh in the state in her tenth grade year. She also won all star at both national showcases. Morgan attended Lausanne her junior year and helped it become a winning program. She was captain and all region player senior year. Morgan continued her career at Limestone College, a top 10 D2 institution, and plans on transferring to Regis University this coming spring.

Lauern Avant '10 joined the lady lynx girls varisty team as an eighth grader along with the honors of being captain, and maintained her status for all 5 years. In 2008, she led Lausanne to the TSSAA Division 2 womens state title with a 32-3 record. That season she averaged 13.5 ppg, 8.3 rpg, 6.5 apg, 6 spg and 1.4 bpg. She was ranked 28 out of 100 for the ESPN 100. She commited to the Lady Vols, then transfered to Rhodes her senior year due to injuries and illnesses. In 2017 she came back to coach for the womens Rhodes basketball team. Now (2019) she is currently coaching the womens Liscomb basketball team here in Tennessee.
